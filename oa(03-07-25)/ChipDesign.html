About Chip Design

Chip design involves creating integrated circuits (ICs) that power modern electronic devices. It covers multiple stages, from defining chip architecture to verifying functionality and optimizing performance for production. Our chip design services cater to businesses across industries like automotive, healthcare, IoT, and AI by offering a comprehensive suite of solutions.

Key Aspects of Chip Design

Architecture Design

Defining the high-level structure and function of the chip, determining components like processors, memory, and communication interfaces.

RTL Design

Writing hardware descriptions using languages like Verilog or VHDL to specify the chipâ€™s digital logic at the register-transfer level.

Verification

Ensuring the chip functions as intended through functional and formal verification processes, such as simulation and Universal Verification Methodology (UVM).

Physical Design

Mapping the RTL design to physical silicon, which includes placement, routing, and timing optimization for area and power efficiency.

Post-Silicon Validation

Testing and debugging the chip after manufacturing to ensure it meets performance, power, and functionality requirements.

Low-Power Design

Implementing techniques to minimize energy consumption, critical for battery-powered and mobile devices.

Advanced Node Expertise

Designing chips at cutting-edge technology nodes (e.g., 7nm, 5nm), which requires addressing challenges like power leakage and variability.

Custom Solutions

Delivering tailor-made chips like ASICs, SoCs, and AI accelerators to meet specific application requirements.
